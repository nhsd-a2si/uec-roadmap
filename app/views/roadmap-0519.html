Urgent & Emergency care: roadmap for digital health and care services

From the Digital Transformation Portfolio

About this roadmap

The Five Year Forward View sets out that to meet rising demand, the NHS must harness the power of information and technology to provide care and services that are convenient for patients, efficient for the NHS and which get people the right care for them as quickly as possible

This roadmap sets out our plans to provide the infrastructure, systems, technology and processes that enable the development of digital urgent care services. We will improve telephone and online triage and provide improved technology to support clinicians, so that treatment is better targeted and patients are referred to the right place, first time

For more information visit https://www.england.nhs.uk/urgent-emergency-care/

Users can speak to a clinician when their triage suggests they might need to ring 999 for a low-acuity ambulance or go to an emergency treatment centre

NHS Pathways Senior Clinician Module v3

New version available to SCM users with streamlined workflow, improved routing for chest pain, possible stroke and allergic reaction symptoms plus expanded Pharmacy and Mental Health options


April - June 2019

NHS 111 online: Help NHS 111 online users who need a prescription

People who urgently need prescription medication can use NHS 111 online to find out what to do next. Their case may be sent electronically for a consultation with a pharmacist.

NHS Pathways Release 17 to support triage

Beta testing and commencement of national roll-out of R17. Clinical improvements to choking, drowning and cold exposure pathways and for unexpected deaths, non mobile babies and injested objects.

CDS API Implementation Guide

A specification to describe how triage / CDSS systems should be built interoperably

Extending contact history (ECH) Discovery

Providing clinicians in UEC settings an at a glance view of recent patient encounters with 111, aiding identification of escalating clinical risk and clinical decision making.

CAD to CAD

Update the messaging specifications for ambulance to ambulance information flow


July - Sept 2019

NHS Pathways Release 18 Live Beta Testing

R18 contains significant changes to high end pathways to support the use of Ambulance resources, Release 18 also contains changes to Care Advice across the entire Pathways suite (750+ pathways) creating a better call experience for patients and reducing call lengths, other changes include changes to clinical pathways in response to user feedback and simplification of the Pathways product to make it easier for call handlers and clinicians to use.

Pathways Clinical Consultation Support (PaCCS) Alpha available

PaCCS is a new clinical consultation support product being developed by NHS Pathways for use by clinicians in 111, 999 and IUC CAS settings. This product will not be algorithm based but will provide structured guidance for clinicians to support their telephone consultation.
